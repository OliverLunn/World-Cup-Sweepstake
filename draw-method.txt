WORLD CUP 2026 SWEEPSTAKE: HOW THE DRAW WORKS


    To ensure fairness and transparency, our sweepstake uses a split-pot randomization system. There is no bias towards drawing first or last. The teams are 
    split into two pots (pot 1 and pot 2). Pot 1 contains the 18 top ranked teams in the world (18 players in the sweepstake) and pot 2 contains the rest of the teams in the tournament.
    Everyone will draw 1 team from each of the pots.


How the draw works:

    Pot 1 contains the top 18 teams in the current FIFA mens world ranking table.
    Pot 2 contains the rest of the teams in the tournament.

POT 1: 18 teams, everyone draws 1 team from this pot.
   
    (Ordered by ranking)
    France, Spain, Argentina, England, Portugal, Brazil, Netherlands, Morocco, Belgium, Germany, Croatia, Colombia, Senegal, Mexico, USA, Uruguay, Japan, Switzerland


POT 2: 30 teams, everyone draws 1 team from this pot.

    (Ordered by region)
    Canada
    Norway, Scotland, Austria, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Sweden, Türkiye, Czechia
    Algeria, Cape Verde, DR Congo, Egypt, Ghana, Ivory Coast, South Africa
    Australia, Iran, Iraq, Jordan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, South Korea, Uzbekistan
    Ecuador, Paraguay
    Panama, Haiti, Curaçao
    New Zealand

Execution of the draw:

    A. Deck Shuffling:
        The moment the draw starts, the system takes Pot 1 and Pot 2 and completely shuffles them independently using a randomized sorting algorithm (the Fisher-Yates shuffle). 
        The algorithm produces an unbiased permutation: every permutation is equally likely. 

    B. Allocation:
        The algorithm moves down the list of our 18 participants one by one. For each person, it draws 2 teams:

            Step 1: It takes the first remaining team from Pot 1 (shuffled) and assigns it to the participant.
            Step 2: It takes the first remaining team from the Pot 2 (shuffled) and assigns it to the same participant.

    C. No card replacement:
        Once a team is drawn from either pot, its removed from the pot.No teams can be drawn twice.
